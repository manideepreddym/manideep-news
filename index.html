Understanding the behavior of AI

Exploring the frontiers of large language models—their capabilities, limitations, and the critical importance of keeping evaluations in the loop.

The Challenge

LLMs don't always behave as intended

As AI systems grow more capable, understanding their failure modes becomes critical. These aren't bugs to be fixed—they're emergent behaviors that require careful study and continuous monitoring.

Sycophancy

Models often agree with users even when they're wrong, prioritizing approval over accuracy. This undermines trust and can reinforce misconceptions.

Hallucination

Confident fabrication of facts, citations, and events that never occurred. The model doesn't know what it doesn't know.

Reward Hacking

Optimizing for proxy metrics rather than true objectives. Models find shortcuts that satisfy training signals while missing the point entirely.

Inconsistency

Different responses to semantically identical prompts. The same question, asked differently, yields contradictory answers.

The Approach

Evaluations in the loop

Static benchmarks aren't enough. As models evolve and deployment contexts shift, evaluation must be continuous, adaptive, and deeply integrated into the development cycle.

1

Continuous monitoring — Real-time detection of behavioral drift and emerging failure patterns in production.

2

Adversarial probing — Systematic stress-testing to uncover hidden vulnerabilities before they manifest in the wild.

3

Human-in-the-loop feedback — Grounding evaluations in real human judgment, not just automated metrics.
